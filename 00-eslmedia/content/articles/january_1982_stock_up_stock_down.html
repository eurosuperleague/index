January 1982

Stock Up, Stock Down: Inter Buys Control, Tottenham Sells Hope

January produced a familiar kind of league confusion: some teams looked more adult, and others managed the opposite.

Graham TrentJanuary 1982 stock board

January produced a familiar sort of league confusion. Some teams improved their position by looking more adult. Others managed the opposite. The trick with a stock column in this league is to avoid confusing a loud night with an actual turn. A proper stock rise changes the room a team walks into. A proper stock drop does the same.

Stock Up: Inter Milan

6-2 January | +91

Inter now look like a team with a weekly identity rather than a streaky scoring act. If this continues, Tier 3 promotion stops being a chase and becomes an expectation.

Stock Down: Tottenham Hotspur

0-6 January | Six-game slide

There is no elegant way to write 0-6. By the time FL Fart beat them by 26, luck had left the conversation.

Stock Up: Real Madrid

5-3 January | +73

Madrid beat Richmond, handled United twice, and finished by taking down Atletico. They are not elite yet. They are no longer easy to dismiss.

Stock Down: Manchester United

1-7 January | -98

A single two-point win over Madrid is not enough cover for a month where United repeatedly gave Tier 1 a stable answer to who belongs at the bottom.

Stock Up: Sheffield United

6-4 January | +52

Sheffield stacked convincing wins over Chelsea, Monaco, and Juventus, and looked like the Tier 2 team most comfortable with promotion pressure.

Stock Down: Valencia

3-6 January | -68

FL Fart beat them by 27. Inter beat them by 22 and then by 24. That is what a team looks like when the upper part of the tier has started to solve it.

The Verdict

The biggest buy is Inter because the month came with both wins and margin. The biggest sell is Tottenham because the losses kept changing shape without changing outcome.
