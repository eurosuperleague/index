Conference League Power Rankings: Inter Takes the Top Line

Inter Milan were the best team in the tier, FL Fart and Barcelona stayed close, and Tottenham made the bottom feel settled in the worst possible way.

Nina ValeJanuary 1982 power board

Tier 3 produced the clearest monthly sorting. Inter Milan were the best team in the tier. FL Fart and Barcelona stayed close enough to matter. Tottenham Hotspur made the bottom of the board feel settled in the worst possible way.

Conference League Board - January Form Check
  1. 1Inter Milan
    14-8 | +91 January | World B. Free
    This is the easiest No. 1 call in the package. Inter collected convincing wins over Barcelona, PSG, Manchester City, and Valencia.
  2. 2FL Fart
    14-7 | David Thompson
    Fourteen wins in twenty-one games still make one of the league's best bodies of work. The 5-2 month kept pressure on the promotion spot.
  3. 3Barcelona
    13-7 | Kelly Tripucka
    The record says 13-7, the latest sim says 5-2, and Tripucka's scoring leaves Barcelona close enough to force Inter into a clean finish.
  4. 4Paris Saint-Germain
    12-11 | Joe Barry Carroll
    PSG are the board's stable fourth-place team: dangerous because Carroll can change the tenor of a week by himself.
  5. 5Valencia
    11-12 | Isiah Thomas
    A 3-6 sim with a minus-68 differential exposed how far Valencia still are from promotion pace.
  6. 6Sporting CP
    10-13 | Buck Williams
    The profile is not enough to support a real climb, but Sporting have remained functionally competent in a tier where that still matters.
  7. 7Manchester City
    8-13 | Calvin Natt
    Natt has an MVP-level statistical case, which says something about how little help he has had.
  8. 8Tottenham Hotspur
    5-16 | John Long
    There is no ranking debate after an 0-6 month. Tottenham kept losing in different ways and still ended in the same place.
The Read

Inter is the riser, Valencia is the faller, and PSG remain good enough to scare people without quite convincing you they can own the tier.
