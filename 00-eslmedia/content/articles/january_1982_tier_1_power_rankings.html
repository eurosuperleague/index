Champions League Power Rankings: Bayern Stays First, Madrid Forces the Conversation

Tier 1 still starts with Bayern and Richmond, but January made the middle of the table much less polite.

Nina ValeJanuary 1982 power board

Tier 1 still belongs to the teams with the best season-long cases, but January shifted the temperature inside the middle of the table. Bayern Munich remain the safest first-place argument. AFC Richmond still own the division's most explosive ceiling. The real movement sits behind them, where Real Madrid and Crystal Palace used the latest sim to look more credible than their records did a month ago.

Champions League Board - January Form Check
  1. 1Bayern Munich
    14-6 | +110 | Bernard King
    The overall case still starts here. Bayern survived a slightly uneven 4-3 month, beat Richmond by 19, and closed January on a two-game winning streak.
  2. 2AFC Richmond
    14-8 | +124 | Magic Johnson
    The 3-4 month complicates the resume, but Magic Johnson keeps the offense above panic level and the 48-point destruction of Brighton remains the biggest single statement in the tier.
  3. 3AC Milan
    13-9 | +50 | Larry Bird
    Milan are dangerous every time the ball finds the right hands. The issue is volatility: heavy losses to Atletico and Palace remind everyone the floor is lower than a contender wants.
  4. 4Real Madrid
    12-11 | +46 | Sidney Moncrief
    This is January's clearest riser. A 5-3 sim and plus-73 monthly differential say the climb is real, not cosmetic.
  5. 5Atletico Madrid
    12-10 | +43 | Adrian Dantley
    The numbers are steady if not overwhelming. Dantley gives them one of the cleanest scoring cases in the tier, but the two-game skid keeps them from climbing.
  6. 6Crystal Palace
    10-12 | -13 | Artis Gilmore
    Palace went 5-2, closed on a four-game winning streak, and beat AC Milan by 18. Their good weeks are starting to look repeatable instead of accidental.
  7. 7Brighton
    9-12 | -76 | Kiki Vandeweghe
    The late win over Richmond matters, but the 103-55 Richmond loss still hangs over the month. This is resistance, not escape.
  8. 8Manchester United
    3-19 | -284 | Walter Davis
    There is no soft version of a 1-7 month with a minus-98 differential. One isolated spike does not repair the bottom of the table.
The Read

Real Madrid is the riser, Brighton is the faller, and AC Milan remains the team most capable of scaring everyone while still leaving you unsure what the next week will look like.
