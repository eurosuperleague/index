Crystal Palace - Fan Voice - 1981 Regular Season

EXCLUSIVE: batsuper Declares Shot-Selection Emergency After Another Alex English Heat Check

An "anonymous" Crystal Palace fan claims to be a regular supporter while delivering suspiciously front-office-grade frustration about Alex English jumpers and Palace's recent game script.

Malik Sparks Fan Interview Column · 1981 Regular Season

South London is in crisis. The tea is cold. The rim is loud. And one "anonymous" Crystal Palace fan named batsuper has entered his villain era with a notebook titled Jump-Shot Accountability and zero indoor voice.

"I have seen things," batsuper said, clutching a clipboard like it owed him money. "I have seen early-clock pull-ups so contested they should require planning permission. We do one action, maybe half an action, then Alex English rises like the shot clock personally insulted him."

To be fair, this is not a one-man tragedy. Palace is 2-6 and has absorbed a rough little run: 115-111 to AC Milan, 107-103 to Atletico Madrid, 107-85 to AFC Richmond, a 105-101 response against Richmond, then 98-86 at Brighton. The result is a fanbase currently speaking in sighs.

"Every game is one smart pass away from hope and one Alex English jumper away from me inventing new vocabulary."

Batsuper insists this is not anti-English propaganda. "Alex can score. Everyone knows Alex can score. I am simply asking for fewer jumpers that begin with confidence and end with four grown men staring at the rafters."

Then he produced what he called the official palace panic scale for Alex English shot attempts:

Palace Panic Scale
Green
Open rhythm jumper, no notes.
Yellow
Mild contest, acceptable chaos.
Red
Step-back over two defenders because "the vibes said yes."
Black
The one where your own bench starts jogging back on defense before the release.
The Core Complaint

Palace's offense has become too allergic to normal possessions for batsuper's taste. "Can we run a pick-and-roll? Can we hit the roller? Can we, for one single stretch of basketball, avoid turning every fourth trip into a difficult Alex English jumper from the outer planets?"

He paused, looked around, and added, "I am not mad. I am focused. This is focused frustration."

When asked what he would change, batsuper offered a three-point reform package:

Three-Point Reform Package
01
Fewer hero pull-ups before the defense even has to work.
02
More paint touches before late-clock improvisation.
03
If two defenders and one nearby cloud are contesting the jumper, pass.
Totally Anonymous

Multiple witnesses confirmed batsuper denied being the Crystal Palace GM "at least seven times," then accidentally referred to the staff as "we" and asked whether "our clip package" was ready by tomorrow morning.

At press time, he remained anonymous, calm, and definitely not refreshing any trade machine at 3:11 a.m.
