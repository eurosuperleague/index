March Tier 3 Power Rankings: FL Fart Took The Line Back

The one-promotion format is brutal again: FL Fart reclaimed first, PSG and City surged, and Inter's case got a little less automatic.

Nina ValeMarch 1982 power board

Tier 3 no longer has one obvious answer. FL Fart are back on top because 6-1, plus-58, and direct wins over Barcelona and Inter are hard to argue with. PSG and Manchester City were also good enough to force real movement. Barcelona and Inter still have strong season profiles, but March made both of them defend their places.

Conference League Board - March Form Check
  1. 1FL Fart
    Up 1 from No. 2 | 25-10 | 6-1 in March | David Thompson
    FL Fart earned the jump. They beat Barcelona by 17, beat Inter twice, finished plus-58, and closed with three straight wins. David Thompson's scoring gives the rise star weight, but the team case is the point: this looked like a promotion club again.
  2. 2Inter Milan
    Down 1 from No. 1 | 23-13 | 4-3 in March | World B. Free
    Inter still own the best point differential in the tier, so this is a narrow drop. The trouble is direct evidence: FL Fart beat them twice in March. World B. Free keeps the floor high, but the board cannot ignore head-to-head pressure.
  3. 3Paris Saint-Germain
    Up 1 from No. 4 | 21-15 | 6-2 in March | Joe Barry Carroll
    PSG had the kind of month that makes the MVP race and promotion race talk to each other. Joe Barry Carroll is now the top Tier 3 ballot name, and the team went 6-2 with a plus-47 margin. The loss at Inter keeps them third; the rise is still real.
  4. 4Manchester City
    Up 1 from No. 5 | 17-19 | 6-3 in March | Calvin Natt, Allen Leavell
    City are no longer just a statistical maybe. They went 6-3, beat Barcelona by 25, and have two MVP-pool names in Calvin Natt and Allen Leavell. The losing record keeps a ceiling on the ranking, but the month was clearly stock up.
  5. 5Barcelona
    Down 2 from No. 3 | 21-14 | 3-5 in March | Kelly Tripucka
    Barcelona fall because 3-5 is not enough in a one-promotion league. Kelly Tripucka still has a loud MVP case and the overall record remains strong, but losses to FL Fart, City, and Valencia made March feel like a missed chance.
  6. 6Valencia
    No change from No. 6 | 15-22 | 2-6 in March | Isiah Thomas
    Valencia stay sixth because the teams below them were worse, not because the month worked. They did beat Inter and Barcelona in close games, which matters, but a 2-6 record and minus-46 margin turn those wins into flashes rather than proof.
  7. 7Tottenham Hotspur
    No change from No. 7 | 10-26 | 3-5 in March | John Long
    Tottenham beat FL Fart and handled Sporting once, so there were useful signs. The season profile is still the second-worst in the tier, and a minus-56 month keeps the board from calling this progress with a straight face.
  8. 8Sporting CP
    Down 1 from No. 7 | 12-25 | 1-6 in March | Buck Williams
    Sporting were the coldest team in the package. A 1-6 month, five straight losses, and an 18-point home loss to FL Fart made the bottom feel earned. The Tottenham win is not enough to soften the fall.
The Read

FL Fart are the current answer, PSG are the best new pressure point, and City are the team that made this race uncomfortable. Barcelona's next month decides whether this was a stumble or a real fade.
