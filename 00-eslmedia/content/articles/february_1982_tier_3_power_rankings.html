February Tier 3 Power Rankings: Inter Still Own The Best Case

Nobody broke the race open in February, but Inter kept the strongest full profile while FL Fart and Barcelona made sure the best promotion fight in the league stayed fully alive.

Nina ValeFebruary 1982 power board

Tier 3 is the hardest board to order because the top three teams all gave you something real. FL Fart still own the best overall record, Barcelona supplied the loudest single result and one of the tightest escapes, and Inter Milan finished with the strongest combined record, differential, and evidence of control. When the margins are this thin, the board has to reward both quality and authority. Inter had the most of both.

Conference League Board - February Form Check
  1. 1Inter Milan
    No change from last board | 19-10 | 5-2 in February | World B. Free
    Inter get the top spot because they looked the most like a promotion team. They beat Barcelona by 25, crushed Sporting by 35, finished the month with the best differential in the tier, and their overall point profile is now the strongest on the board.
  2. 2FL Fart
    No change from last board | 19-9 | 5-2 in February | David Thompson, Larry Drew
    The best record in the tier still counts for a lot. FL Fart were another 5-2, kept a strong scoring margin, and remain fully level in the promotion chase. The only reason they are second is that Inter's month felt a little more forceful.
  3. 3Barcelona
    No change from last board | 18-9 | 5-2 in February | Kelly Tripucka
    Barcelona had the month's biggest blowout when they demolished Valencia by 39, and they also owned the closest finish when they edged Manchester City by one. That duality is why they stay near the top: overwhelming on their best night, slightly too volatile to own first.
  4. 4Paris Saint-Germain
    No change from last board | 15-13 | 3-2 in February | Joe Barry Carroll
    PSG still live in the second tier of this race rather than the first, but the team remains respectable because Joe Barry Carroll keeps producing star-level offense and the month ended with another winning sim.
  5. 5Manchester City
    Up 1 from No. 6 | 11-16 | 3-3 in February | Calvin Natt
    This is the still-unsure team. Manchester City were .500 in the month, but the underlying margin was much better than the record. Calvin Natt's case keeps them dangerous enough to punish a wobble above them.
  6. 6Valencia
    Down 1 from No. 5 | 13-16 | 2-4 in February | Isiah Thomas
    Valencia took one of the loudest beatings of the month and posted a brutal minus-12.8 average margin. They are only this high because the teams behind them were even less convincing.
  7. 7Tottenham Hotspur
    Up 1 from No. 8 | 7-21 | 2-5 in February | John Long
    The results are still bad, but Tottenham at least looked less broken than Sporting. This is faint praise, not momentum, yet it matters at the bottom.
  8. 8Sporting CP
    Down 1 from No. 7 | 11-19 | 1-6 in February | Buck Williams
    Sporting beat Inter once at the end of the month, but that cannot cover a 1-6 sim and the worst average margin in the tier. They spent February being acted upon more than shaping anything themselves.
The Read

Inter hold the strongest promotion profile, FL Fart and Barcelona are still close enough to flip the whole line in one good week, and the rest of Tier 3 is mostly fighting to avoid becoming scenery around that race.
