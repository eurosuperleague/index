Conference League Awards Predictions: This Tier Is Messy, So Let's Be Honest

The bottom tier is volatile, chaotic, and exactly the kind of environment where star guards and defensive monsters can seize an award race before the room even realizes what happened.

Damon Cross Conference League awards preview

This is the most difficult awards board in the entire pyramid, because the Conference League does not do us the courtesy of being stable. But even in the noise, I know what I trust: I trust Isiah Thomas to be the most undeniable offensive engine in the tier. World B. Free has the bigger raw overall number, yes, but I am not putting my MVP chip on a player attached to a club already wobbling at the bottom of the early form picture. I want the star who can drag a team upward, command the ball every night, and make the award conversation impossible to avoid. That is Isiah.

MVP Prediction
Isiah Thomas, Valencia
He has the biggest blend of star profile, control, and headline-making ability in the tier. If Valencia rises at all, his candidacy writes itself.
Defensive Player Of The Year
Alton Lister, FL Fart
Ninety-five block rating, real interior authority, and a club already flirting with the top line. I am not complicating this one.

Why This Board Can Move Fast

The Conference League is a pressure cooker for awards because there is less insulation here. One month can swing the entire conversation. If Manchester City keeps winning, Allen Leavell is going to become a darling because people love attaching guard play to the best record in a tier. If Paris Saint-Germain hardens up, Joe Barry Carroll and Larry Smith are both going to steal oxygen from somebody else's campaign. If Inter Milan ever gets off the canvas, World B. Free is talented enough to crash this whole discussion.

But today, in this moment, I am trusting role clarity. Isiah Thomas is the cleanest star lead in the division. Alton Lister is the cleanest defensive force. Buck Williams belongs on the first team because toughness travels, rebounding travels, and Sporting is not going to survive without him being central to everything serious they do.

This is also the tier where voters could get seduced by record faster than talent. That is why I kept Kelly Tripucka and World B. Free just outside the first team to open. They are real names. They are dangerous names. But until their clubs prove they can live higher in the food chain, I am siding with players whose teams are more likely to make the awards argument feel credible every single week. Messy league, clean logic. That is the board.
