MVP Race - December 1981

Magic holds, Moses owns, and Tier 3 starts a fight

December tightened the MVP board without making it tidy: one leader survives a Player of the Month challenge, one leader has no real challenger, and one tier is now an argument about what value means.

The December MVP race has one simple rule before the ballot starts: availability matters. Every candidate below cleared the 60 percent threshold, which means at least nine games in his tier. That removes the decorative cases and leaves the useful ones: players who have carried the scoring table, changed the standings table, or forced the awards table to remember their names.

The state of the race is not the same in all three tiers. Tier 1 is a real debate between Magic Johnson's complete profile and Adrian Dantley's December surge. Tier 2 is Moses Malone standing in the middle of the room while everyone else tries to find a different category to win. Tier 3 is the hardest ballot because Calvin Natt has the loudest individual season, but Manchester City's record keeps asking whether loud is enough.

The deciding factor this month: production gets the door open, but team leverage decides who gets the best seat.
Tier 1 Ballot
Champions League MVP Race
  1. 1Magic Johnson, AFC Richmond
    24.7 PPG | 6.6 RPG | 6.3 APG | .676 TS | +10.1 plus-minus
    Dantley took December Player of the Month, but Magic still owns the best full case. Richmond is 11-4, he has the tier's best PER in this pool, and his efficiency plus creation gives him the cleanest blend of box score and team value.
  2. 2Adrian Dantley, Atletico Madrid
    29.1 PPG | 5.0 RPG | 4.1 APG | December Player of the Month
    The strongest scoring case in Tier 1 is no longer just a heater; it has an award stamp. The hesitation is team context and a negative plus-minus, but Atletico's 6-3 December makes the argument much harder to dismiss.
  3. 3George Gervin, AC Milan
    28.9 PPG | .622 TS | 12/27 Player of the Week
    Gervin climbed because AC Milan climbed. The Ice Man gave the league a 37-point weekly award file, Milan finished December on a W3, and the scoring is too efficient to treat as empty volume.
  4. 4Larry Bird, AC Milan
    24.6 PPG | 8.9 RPG | 4.2 APG | .432 from three | +7.1 plus-minus
    Bird has the more complete Milan profile, which is why this is close with Gervin. The lack of a December award keeps him fourth for now, but his rebound-pass-shoot line is built to move up if Milan's surge keeps holding.
  5. 5Kevin McHale, Bayern Munich
    21.5 PPG | 9.7 RPG | .645 TS | +10.8 plus-minus
    Bayern owns the top of the Champions League table, and McHale is the best individual bridge between that record and the ballot. He is not the biggest scorer here, but 21.5 and 9.7 on that efficiency gives the leaders a real MVP voice.

Sidney Moncrief and Artis Gilmore are the next two names. Moncrief has the steals and shooting; Gilmore has the defensive burden. Both need their teams to give the numbers better lighting.

Tier 2 Ballot
Europa League MVP Race
  1. 1Moses Malone, Benfica
    37.8 PPG | 12.8 RPG | 24.5 PER | December Player of the Month
    This is still the strongest statistical case in the league. Moses leads Tier 2 in points and rebounds, won December Player of the Month, and took two December weekly awards. Benfica's record is good enough that nobody gets to call this a hollow avalanche.
  2. 2Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Juventus
    31.1 PPG | 8.7 RPG | 3.3 BPG | promotion-line team
    Kareem is the only Tier 2 challenger with enough scoring and rim protection to make Moses look over his shoulder. Juventus sits on the promotion line, and that keeps his 31-and-3-blocks profile alive.
  3. 3Mike Mitchell, Marseille
    24.9 PPG | 7.6 RPG | .615 TS | +10.6 plus-minus
    Marseille went 7-0 in December and owns the best overall record in the tier. Mitchell is the cleanest scorer on that machine, so he gets the first Marseille ballot slot even without the loudest award resume.
  4. 4Robert Parish, Marseille
    17.1 PPG | 12.1 RPG | 2.1 BPG | +12.0 plus-minus
    Parish is the team-context candidate in its purest form. The counting points are lower, but Marseille's 13-1 record and his plus-minus make the defensive spine impossible to ignore.
  5. 5Steve Johnson, Sheffield United
    23.1 PPG | 7.5 RPG | .684 TS | 20.5 PER
    Johnson's efficiency keeps him ahead of the crowded second group. Sheffield is still 9-5 and second in the tier, but the late two-game slide leaves him needing January wins to protect this spot.

Dan Issel and Jim Paxson both have award arguments. Issel's December week matters, Paxson's November signal still matters, but this ballot leaned harder into season-long value and promotion gravity.

Tier 3 Ballot
Conference League MVP Race
  1. 1Kelly Tripucka, Barcelona
    32.1 PPG | 24.0 PER | .605 TS | Barcelona 8-5
    Tripucka keeps the top line by a thread because Barcelona is still one of the tier's best records and his scoring leads the league. The caveat is real: a 2-4 December made this race vulnerable.
  2. 2Calvin Natt, Manchester City
    30.8 PPG | 9.5 RPG | 4.0 EWA | December Player of the Month
    Natt has the loudest individual file in Tier 3 and might be the biggest riser of the month. The only reason he is not first is Manchester City at 5-9 with a negative margin. That is not a small caveat; it is the entire argument.
  3. 3David Thompson, FL Fart
    29.4 PPG | .630 TS | FL Fart 9-5
    Thompson has the best team-context counterpunch. FL Fart leads Tier 3, his efficiency is elite, and the only thing keeping him third is that Tripucka and Natt have been louder in the season-long scoring conversation.
  4. 4Joe Barry Carroll, Paris Saint-Germain
    27.4 PPG | 7.8 RPG | 24.2 PER | +4.4 plus-minus
    Carroll has the best PER in the pool and a December weekly award, but PSG is 7-8 and still drifting around the middle of the table. The player case is excellent. The team case is unfinished.
  5. 5World B. Free, Inter Milan
    27.6 PPG | 4.1 APG | 12/27 Player of the Week | Inter 8-6
    Free gets the fifth spot because Inter has the tier's best point differential and he owns the latest weekly award. Isiah Thomas, Buck Williams, and Larry Drew are close enough to make this uncomfortable.

The biggest riser is Natt, because December gave his numbers an award and forced the ballot to respect Manchester City's best player even while questioning the team. The strongest statistical case is Moses, because 37.8 and 12.8 with the monthly award is not a race so much as a jurisdiction claim.

The player whose numbers need the most team context is also Natt. That is the Tier 3 problem in one sentence. If City starts winning, he can pass everyone. If City keeps losing, Tripucka, Thompson, Carroll, Free, Isiah, Buck, and Drew will all keep finding ways to make the ballot less friendly.
