Promotion and Relegation Watch - November 1981

The lines are already cruel

November has given the league four pressure points: a Tier 1 trapdoor, a Tier 2 sprint, a survival mess, and Barcelona's narrow grip on the only Tier 3 promotion place.

Graham TrentNovember 1981 race watch

The first rule of a race watch is to respect the line before inventing the mood. Tier 1 sends two down. Tier 2 sends two up and one down. Tier 3 sends one up. That structure is already doing real work, because the current tables have produced pressure without needing any melodrama added to them.

Tier 1 Relegation
Manchester United and Crystal Palace are in the trapdoor seats
Two down | United 2-5, Palace 2-6
United has the worst point differential in Tier 1 at minus-100, while Palace is one game deeper into the loss column. Atletico Madrid and Brighton are not clear, but they at least have slightly less damage on the page.

Crystal Palace complicates the story by owning an upset of AFC Richmond, which is exactly the kind of result that makes a bad team annoying rather than hopeless. United does not have that luxury right now. Three straight losses make the pressure feel earned.

Tier 2 Promotion
Sheffield United and Marseille have the line, Benfica has the chase
Two up | Sheffield 6-1, Marseille 6-1, Benfica 5-2
Sheffield and Marseille are both above the promotion line by one win. Benfica is close enough to make this live, and Chelsea's plus-63 differential says fourth place is not a comfortable ceiling.

This is the cleanest race in the league because it has quality at the top and credible pressure directly behind it. Sheffield's W5 and Marseille's W4 are the current headline. Benfica is the reminder that one result can change the tone.

Tier 2 Relegation
Aston Villa is still waiting for the first win
One down | Villa 0-8, Monaco 1-5, Ajax 2-4
Aston Villa sits on the relegation line because there is no softer way to describe 0-8. Monaco is only one win clear with a worse differential, and Ajax has lost four straight.

Villa is the obvious danger name. Monaco is the hidden concern. Ajax is the club that should be nervous because a slide can turn a cushion into a trap very quickly.

Tier 3 Promotion
Barcelona has the only ticket for now
One up | Barcelona 6-1, FL Fart 5-2, Inter Milan 4-2
Barcelona has the record. FL Fart has the better differential. Inter Milan has a strong enough per-game profile to keep the top two honest.

One promotion spot makes this the league's harshest race. Barcelona has earned the line, but FL Fart is close enough that every narrow Barcelona win and every big FL Fart result will get measured against each other.
