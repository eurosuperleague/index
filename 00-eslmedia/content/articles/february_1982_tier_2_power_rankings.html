Tier 2 Power Rankings: Benfica Roars Past The Board, Marseille Crashes

The top of Tier 2 now has two clear power centers, and February turned Marseille from the league's safest middle-tier team into the board's biggest drop.

Nina ValeFebruary 1982 power board

Overall record still matters in this league, but February was too loud to ignore. Benfica had the best month on the entire map, Sheffield United kept winning like a club that expects promotion, Juventus strengthened its argument, and Marseille used a 1-6 spiral to blow up any claim that the top of Tier 2 was settled.

Europa League Board - February Form Check
  1. 1Benfica
    Up 2 from No. 3 | 20-9 | 7-1 in February | Moses Malone
    This month belongs to Benfica. They went 7-1, posted a plus-110 differential, and beat Marseille by 37 while Moses Malone kept producing like the tier's clearest superstar. Same record as Sheffield, better month, louder statement.
  2. 2Sheffield United
    No change from last board | 20-9 | 5-0 in February | Rickey Green, Steve Johnson
    No one has a cleaner promotion file. Sheffield did not play as many games as Benfica, but a perfect 5-0 month with a double-digit average margin is exactly what control looks like. They are first in the table and still feel like there is more room left.
  3. 3Juventus
    Up 2 from No. 5 | 16-12 | 4-1 in February | Kareem Abdul-Jabbar
    This is the riser most likely to matter next month. Kareem still gives Juventus the tier's other monster individual case, and the 4-1 sim with an 8.8 average margin is enough to treat them as a live promotion threat rather than a distant one.
  4. 4Chelsea
    No change from last board | 14-13 | 3-2 in February | Jack Sikma
    Chelsea remain the line team because they did enough to avoid giving their place away. The month was not dominant, but it was steady enough to keep them above the real danger behind them. Sikma still makes the core case respectable.
  5. 5Marseille
    Down 4 from No. 1 | 17-11 | 1-6 in February | Robert Parish
    This is the hardest fall on any February board. Marseille still have the third-best record in the tier, which is the only reason they are not lower, but a 1-6 month with a minus-98 differential and an ugly loss to Ajax wrecked the illusion of safety.
  6. 6Ajax
    Up 1 from No. 7 | 10-19 | 2-5 in February | Cliff Robinson
    The record stays ugly, but the shape of the month mattered. Ajax closed with the upset of the month against Marseille and looked more alive than the clubs behind them. They are not good yet. They are just no longer inert.
  7. 7Monaco
    Down 1 from No. 6 | 11-16 | 3-4 in February | Purvis Short
    Monaco still own the weaker season body of work among the middle teams. The month had some bite, including the win over Benfica, but a minus-42 February differential keeps them in the maybe-interesting, not actually-trusted, range.
  8. 8Aston Villa
    No change from last board | 5-24 | 0-6 in February | Bill Laimbeer
    Villa had the roughest month in the tier and did nothing to suggest escape is close. Until the results start looking less one-sided, this spot is automatic.
The Read

Benfica and Sheffield are the only teams that feel fully promotion-worthy right now, Juventus are charging into the picture, and Marseille just turned the top of Tier 2 from stable to combustible.
