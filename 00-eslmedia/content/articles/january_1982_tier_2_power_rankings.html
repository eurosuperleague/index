Europa League Power Rankings: Marseille Holds First With Less Comfort

The best season-long resume no longer belongs to the most convincing current team, which is exactly why Tier 2 has turned dangerous.

Nina ValeJanuary 1982 power board

Tier 2 is the most unstable board in the league right now because the strongest season-long resume no longer belongs to the most convincing current team. Marseille still own the best overall record. Sheffield United played the best sustained month among the promotion contenders. Chelsea and Benfica both did enough to keep the line fluid.

Europa League Board - January Form Check
  1. 1Marseille
    16-5 | Robert Parish
    The first-place vote survives because 16-5 still matters, but a 3-4 month and a three-game losing streak make this the least comfortable No. 1 in the package.
  2. 2Sheffield United
    15-9 | Steve Johnson
    This is the best current-form case in the tier. Sheffield went 6-4, won big repeatedly, and posted a plus-52 month even after cooling late.
  3. 3Benfica
    13-8 | Moses Malone
    A 4-2 sim with a plus-64 differential demands upward attention. Moses Malone is the single best player in the tier and the clearest force inside the promotion race.
  4. 4Chelsea
    11-11 | Jack Sikma
    The overall record is only even, but January improved the argument. A 5-3 month and a current promotion-line spot say this team is doing more than treading water.
  5. 5Juventus
    12-11 | Kareem Abdul-Jabbar
    Kareem guarantees relevance, but the team case softened after a 4-5 sim and a hard late loss to Sheffield.
  6. 6Monaco
    8-12 | Purvis Short
    Monaco are the partial-believer test. The record resists trust, but the four-game winning streak officially makes them annoying.
  7. 7Ajax
    8-14 | Cliff Robinson
    The January read is better than the season summary. A three-game winning streak keeps them alive in both directions.
  8. 8Aston Villa
    5-18 | Larry Nance
    The upset of Marseille deserves a nod, not a pardon. One loud win is not the same as evidence.
The Read

Chelsea is the riser, Marseille is the uncomfortable faller, and Monaco is the team whose momentum is real even if the full-season profile still argues back.
