Month In Review - March 1982

March 1982 Month in Review: Milan Changed The Trust Test

March was the month where form started arguing with reputation: Milan surged, Benfica got hit, Villa woke up, and Tier 3 became a three-team stress test.

Graham TrentMarch 1982 monthly review

March did not have one story. It had a correction. AC Milan stopped looking like a talented maybe and started looking like a team with proof. Chelsea reminded Benfica that the Tier 2 leader can still be dragged into a fight. Aston Villa, of all teams, supplied the weirdest useful jolt. And FL Fart turned the Tier 3 promotion race back into a line they control.

Team Of The Month
AC Milan
6-2 | +72 | W4 | Beat Richmond and Bayern
The case is clean. Milan had the strongest month in Tier 1, beat two of the clubs above them on the February board, and closed with four straight wins. The 111-98 win over Bayern was the result that changed perception; the 119-97 win over Atletico made it feel like a trend.
The Loudest Game
Chelsea 118, Benfica 81
March 12 | Biggest blowout of the sim | 37-point margin
Benfica still finished the month in control, but Chelsea gave the league a result it could not ignore. A 37-point win over the Tier 2 leader does not make Chelsea the favorite. It does make them impossible to dismiss as a soft line team.
The Weirdest Useful Result
Aston Villa 115, Benfica 104
March 2 | Upset of the month
Villa's season is still ugly, but March gave them evidence. They upset Benfica, then edged Sheffield 113-112 the next night, then later beat Ajax by 29. That is not enough to erase a relegation file. It is enough to say the bottom of Tier 2 is no longer inert.
The Tightest Finish
Aston Villa 113, Sheffield United 112
March 3 | One-point game
This was the closest game of the month and the perfect image of March's Tier 2 mood. Sheffield are still a promotion-quality team, but a one-point loss to Villa showed how quickly the race can turn from clean to irritated.
The Race That Changed

Tier 3 belongs here because FL Fart went 6-1, beat Barcelona, beat Inter twice, and pushed PSG and Manchester City into chase mode. Inter still own a massive season differential. Barcelona still has Kelly Tripucka. But March rewarded the teams that won the month, not the teams that entered it with the neatest story.

The Cold Month

Sporting CP had the roughest March at 1-6 with a five-game losing streak. Atletico Madrid were almost as concerning because their 2-7 month came with a minus-111 margin and a fall from Tier 1 comfort into survival pressure. Those are not just bad records; those are identity problems.
