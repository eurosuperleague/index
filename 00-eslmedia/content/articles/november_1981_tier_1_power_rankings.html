Champions League Power Rankings: Richmond owns November

AFC Richmond has the strongest total case in Tier 1, but Bayern Munich's surge and Real Madrid's recovery keep the top of the board honest.

Nina ValeNovember 1981 power board

The top of Tier 1 is not complicated if the ranking starts with results. AFC Richmond went 6-2, outscored opponents by 79, and still owns the best blend of record, margin, and roster gravity in the division. Magic Johnson gives Richmond the highest ceiling on the board, but this is not just a star-player vote. The team has the file to match the name.

Bayern Munich is the only club with a strong argument to jump them. Five straight wins, a plus-63 differential, and Bernard King leading a cleaner month than almost anyone else means Bayern has moved from respectable to dangerous. The one thing keeping Richmond in front is volume: more wins, more total separation, and a broader resume.

Champions League Board
  1. 1AFC Richmond
    6-2 | +79 | Magic Johnson
    Richmond's only real flaw is that the month ended with an eight-point loss to Bayern. Everything before that still says No. 1.
  2. 2Bayern Munich
    5-1 | +63 | Bernard King
    Bayern is the riser. The five-game winning streak is real, and the 33-point win over Manchester United gave the month a proper statement.
  3. 3Real Madrid
    4-2 | +41 | Sidney Moncrief
    Three straight wins moved Madrid out of the middle. The AC Milan loss still matters, but the current direction is upward.
  4. 4AC Milan
    4-3 | +17 | Larry Bird
    Milan has enough quality to beat anyone, but the 27-point loss to Richmond keeps the ceiling conversation slightly uncomfortable.
  5. 5Brighton
    2-4 | -17 | Kiki Vandeweghe
    Brighton is still underwater, but the latest win over AC Milan keeps them above the deeper danger tier for now.
  6. 6Atletico Madrid
    2-4 | -21 | James Donaldson
    Atletico is close enough to Brighton to argue, but the profile is thinner and the margin case is slightly worse.
  7. 7Crystal Palace
    2-6 | -62 | Artis Gilmore
    Palace has the upset of Richmond in its pocket. It also has six losses, which is why that upset reads as a warning rather than a rescue.
  8. 8Manchester United
    2-5 | -100 | Walter Davis
    United has lost three straight and owns the worst point differential in the tier. This is the easiest bottom call on the board.
Movement That Matters

The riser is Bayern. The faller is Crystal Palace, a team that can still produce a serious result but has not strung together enough steady basketball to escape the bottom two. The team I am still unsure about is Brighton, because the latest win is useful but the larger record still asks for caution.
