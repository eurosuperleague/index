March Stock Up, Stock Down: Milan And FL Fart Bought The Month

This is not just a best-teams, worst-teams list. It is about whose March changed the way their season feels.

Graham TrentMarch 1982 stock report

The cleanest stock calls are not always the table leaders. March rewarded teams that changed belief. AC Milan turned a trust problem into a top-three Tier 1 case. FL Fart took back control of the one-promotion race. PSG moved from respectable to genuinely dangerous. On the other side, Sporting CP and Atletico Madrid did not merely lose games; they gave the league a reason to mark them down.

Stock Up
AC Milan
6-2 | +72 | W4 | Best win: 111-98 vs Bayern
Milan were the month's clearest buy because the wins had substance. They beat Richmond, beat Bayern, beat Palace twice, and closed by putting 119 on Atletico. Larry Bird and George Gervin already gave the roster credibility; March finally gave it a result profile to match.
FL Fart
6-1 | +58 | W3 | Beat Barcelona and Inter twice
This is how a one-promotion team grabs the room. FL Fart did not just beat weaker teams. They beat Barcelona by 17, won twice at Inter, and finished the month by holding off Manchester City. David Thompson's MVP case now sits inside the strongest Tier 3 team case.
Paris Saint-Germain
6-2 | +47 | Joe Barry Carroll leads the Tier 3 MVP board
PSG had the slightly quieter rise, but it may matter just as much. A 6-2 month put them back inside the promotion conversation, and Carroll's 29.4 points, 8.0 rebounds, and 27.8 PER make the surge feel driven by a real star rather than a soft schedule.
Stock Down
Sporting CP
1-6 | -39 | L5 | Coldest team of the month
Sporting are the obvious sell because the month offered no cover. One win, five straight losses, and an 18-point home loss to FL Fart pushed them below Tottenham on the power board. In a one-promotion tier, this is not just bad form. It is disappearance.
Atletico Madrid
2-7 | -111 | L7 | Worst Tier 1 month
Atletico are here because the fall was violent. They entered March as a top-three Tier 1 power-ranking team and left it on a seven-game losing streak. The win at Real Madrid was useful, but the 33-point loss to Bayern and 22-point loss to Milan tell the truth louder.
Valencia
2-6 | -46 | Four close games | Beat Inter and Barcelona
Valencia are not here because nothing good happened. They beat Inter by three and Barcelona by one, which is exactly why the month is frustrating. The broader file was still 2-6 with a minus-46 margin and a 19-point loss at Barcelona. Good teams turn those flashes into a climb; Valencia turned them into trivia.
