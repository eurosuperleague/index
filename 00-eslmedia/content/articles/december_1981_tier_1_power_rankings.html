Champions League Power Rankings: Bayern Takes the Top Line

Bayern Munich finally has enough evidence to pass AFC Richmond, but the top four have turned Tier 1 into a real argument.

Nina ValeDecember 1981 power board

The November board belonged to AFC Richmond because the results, margin, and Magic Johnson gravity all pointed the same direction. December changes the first line, but not by much. Bayern Munich is 10-3 overall, plus-115, and just went 5-2 with a plus-52 latest sim. Richmond is still excellent at 11-4, but Bayern's cleaner efficiency profile is strong enough to flip the top two.

This is not a demotion of Richmond as much as a recognition of Bayern's complete case. Bernard King gives the attack a reliable star line, the team has not played a close game all month, and the 22-point win at Real Madrid gave the file a result that travels. The doubt is real too: Crystal Palace beat Bayern this month, which is why the gap at No. 1 is narrow rather than declarative.

Champions League Board - Movement From November
  1. 1Bayern Munich
    Up 1 | 10-3 | +115 | Bernard King
    The best margin profile in Tier 1 moves Bayern above Richmond. The Palace loss keeps this from being comfortable, but the overall file is now the cleanest in the division.
  2. 2AFC Richmond
    Down 1 | 11-4 | +106 | Magic Johnson
    Richmond still owns the most wins and a four-game streak. The only reason they move down is that Bayern's win rate and point differential are slightly sharper.
  3. 3AC Milan
    Up 1 | 9-6 | +64 | Larry Bird
    Milan's December was uneven but productive: 5-3, plus-47, and a 27-point hit on Manchester United. Larry Bird gives them the kind of floor that makes the early panic look dated.
  4. 4Atletico Madrid
    Up 2 | 8-7 | +8 | James Donaldson
    Atletico is the riser. A 6-3 month, a win over Bayern, and positive latest-sim margin move them ahead of the middle group.
  5. 5Real Madrid
    Down 2 | 7-8 | -27 | Sidney Moncrief
    The fall is deserved. Madrid went 3-6 this sim with a minus-68 margin, and the previous No. 3 case has been replaced by relegation-line anxiety.
  6. 6Brighton
    Down 1 | 6-8 | -23 | Kiki Vandeweghe
    Brighton is not falling apart, but four straight losses changed the tone. The record still beats the bottom two; the direction does not.
  7. 7Crystal Palace
    No change | 5-10 | -57 | Artis Gilmore
    Palace beat Bayern, which matters. The problem is everything around it: 5-10 overall and still buried below the safety conversation.
  8. 8Manchester United
    No change | 2-12 | -186 | Walter Davis
    Seven straight losses and a minus-86 December make United the easiest ranking on the board. The evidence has stopped whispering.
The Next Change Trigger

Richmond can reclaim first with one clean week because the gap is tiny. Atletico can climb again if the Bayern win becomes part of a pattern rather than a single headline. The team I am least settled on is Real Madrid: the roster still says middle, but the current form says danger.
