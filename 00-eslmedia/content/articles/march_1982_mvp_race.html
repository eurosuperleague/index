MVP Race - March 1982

March MVP Race: Carroll Crashes The Top Line

Magic Johnson and Moses Malone still hold the cleanest leads, but Tier 3 changed at the top and several ballots now have visible movement.

Damon CrossMarch 1982 MVP race

The March MVP board is not a stat sort. Team context moved the race. Magic Johnson holds Tier 1 because Richmond took the power-ranking lead. Moses Malone remains too loud to catch in Tier 2 even after Benfica's weird month. Tier 3 is where the real change happened: Joe Barry Carroll jumped Kelly Tripucka because PSG surged while Barcelona stumbled.

Tier 1 MVP Race
  1. 1Magic Johnson, AFC Richmond
    No change from No. 1 | 24.0 PPG | 6.3 RPG | 7.4 APG | 30.1 PER
    Magic stays first because the production and team context still meet cleanly. Richmond went 5-2 in March and moved to No. 1 on the power board, while Magic remains the tier's best creator and most complete value case.
  2. 2Larry Bird, AC Milan
    No change from No. 2 | 24.5 PPG | 8.6 RPG | 3.9 APG | .626 TS
    Bird stays second, but the case is stronger than it was a month ago. Milan went 6-2, closed on a four-game run, and now look like a real Tier 1 threat. That team jump gives Bird's numbers better weight.
  3. 3Adrian Dantley, Atletico Madrid
    No change from No. 3 | 26.1 PPG | 24.2 PER | 31.0 usage
    Dantley holds third on scoring force, not team momentum. Atletico's 2-7 month and seven-game skid keep this from rising. The warning is obvious: another bad team month and the ballot can no longer protect him.
  4. 4George Gervin, AC Milan
    New entry | 26.3 PPG | 21.7 PER | .613 TS
    Gervin enters because Milan's rise created room for a second candidate. He is scoring more than Bird, and the 6-2 month makes the two-star Milan argument feel legitimate rather than indulgent.
  5. 5Sidney Moncrief, Real Madrid
    Down 1 from No. 4 | 23.1 PPG | 4.3 RPG | 3.2 APG | 21.2 PER
    Moncrief drops one only because Gervin forced his way in. The team context actually improved: Real went 5-1 and won five straight. That keeps Moncrief on the ballot even if the raw case is a shade quieter than the top four.
Tier 2 MVP Race
  1. 1Moses Malone, Benfica
    No change from No. 1 | 37.2 PPG | 12.4 RPG | 33.4 PER | 17.1 EWA
    This is still the easiest first-place vote in the package. Benfica had a noisy month, but Malone's statistical lead is so large that the race starts with everyone else arguing for second.
  2. 2Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Juventus
    No change from No. 2 | 31.7 PPG | 8.6 RPG | 28.4 PER
    Kareem remains the only real statistical challenger. Juventus improved their promotion case in March, and the 111-76 win over Chelsea helps his ballot more than a quiet box-score week would.
  3. 3Jack Sikma, Chelsea
    Up 1 from No. 4 | 23.1 PPG | 9.1 RPG | 3.3 APG | 22.4 PER
    Sikma moves up because Chelsea's March had the biggest single punch in Tier 2: a 37-point win over Benfica. He is not catching Malone, but he is the cleanest all-around case on a team that just proved it can dent the leader.
  4. 4Robert Parish, Marseille
    New entry | 19.5 PPG | 11.9 RPG | 3.1 APG | 21.6 PER
    Parish enters because Marseille steadied itself. A 4-3 month with a plus-34 margin and a win over Juventus put his defensive-center value back in the race. This is not a glamour case; it is a structure case.
  5. 5Steve Johnson, Sheffield United
    Down 2 from No. 3 | 22.3 PPG | 8.0 RPG | 25.8 PER | .691 TS
    Johnson's efficiency is still excellent, but Sheffield's 3-4 March cost him. The candidate did not collapse. The team leverage softened, and in this tier that is enough to slide two spots.
Tier 3 MVP Race
  1. 1Joe Barry Carroll, Paris Saint-Germain
    Up 1 from No. 2 | 29.4 PPG | 8.0 RPG | 1.9 BPG | 27.8 PER
    Carroll takes the top spot because PSG finally gave his numbers the right team context. A 6-2 month pushed PSG back into the promotion fight, and Carroll's two-way profile is now the cleanest blend of star and stakes in Tier 3.
  2. 2Calvin Natt, Manchester City
    Up 3 from No. 5 | 28.7 PPG | 9.9 RPG | 25.0 PER
    Natt is the biggest riser on the whole ballot. City went 6-3, beat Barcelona by 25, and started to make his box-score case matter. The team record still caps him, but the March movement is real.
  3. 3Kelly Tripucka, Barcelona
    Down 2 from No. 1 | 31.9 PPG | 5.3 RPG | 28.3 PER | .608 TS
    Tripucka's individual case is still loud, but Barcelona's 3-5 month knocks him off the top line. That is the cost of a one-promotion race: when the team stumbles, the MVP case feels less urgent.
  4. 4David Thompson, FL Fart
    Down 1 from No. 3 | 29.9 PPG | 27.5 PER | .616 TS
    Thompson drops only because Carroll and Natt surged harder individually. The team case is excellent: FL Fart went 6-1 and own the line. Another month like that could easily push him back into the top two.
  5. 5World B. Free, Inter Milan
    Down 1 from No. 4 | 27.1 PPG | 4.6 APG | 24.6 PER | 5.0 plus-minus
    Free stays in because Inter's season profile is still strong and his plus-minus remains the best of this group. He drops because March gave FL Fart the head-to-head wins and PSG the louder rise.
