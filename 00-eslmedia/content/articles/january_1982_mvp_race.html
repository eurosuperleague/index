MVP Race - January 1982

Magic still leads, Moses still looms, and Tier 3 gets loud

January did not produce one universal favorite. It produced three different tests of value, context, and momentum.

Damon CrossJanuary 1982 MVP race
28.3Magic PER
37.5Moses PPG
30.7Carroll PPG

Stop pretending these ballots are settled. They are not settled. They are just clearer than they were a month ago. January produced three different tests: can the best player also carry the best story, can a great stat line survive a shaky team context, and how much weight should recent momentum carry when the raw production is already obscene?

Production gets the door open. Team leverage decides who gets the best seat.
Tier 1 Ballot
Champions League MVP Race
  1. 1Magic Johnson, AFC Richmond
    23.6 PPG | 6.5 RPG | 6.7 APG | 28.3 PER | .681 TS
    Richmond still own one of Tier 1's best records, Johnson is the most complete offensive engine in the tier, and nobody combines efficiency, playmaking, and team consequence this cleanly.
  2. 2Larry Bird, AC Milan
    23.4 PPG | 7.8 RPG | 4.0 APG | .438 3PT
    Bird's argument is precision. His shot profile keeps him within striking distance if Milan steady the team result.
  3. 3Adrian Dantley, Atletico Madrid
    26.2 PPG | 31.2 usage
    Dantley has the loudest pure scoring number among the top candidates. The hesitation is team context on a ballot this tight.
  4. 4George Gervin, AC Milan
    26.6 PPG | elite scoring pressure
    Gervin is impossible to ignore, but internal competition with Bird keeps him from climbing higher.
  5. 5Sidney Moncrief, Real Madrid
    23.5 PPG | 4.6 RPG | 3.1 APG | 1.7 STL
    Moncrief is the candidate most likely to jump if Real Madrid keep rising while others wobble.
Tier 2 Ballot
Europa League MVP Race
  1. 1Moses Malone, Benfica
    37.5 PPG | 13.3 RPG | 2.0 BLK | 28.7 PER
    This is not close right now. If Benfica keep pressure on the promotion line, Malone stays first.
  2. 2Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Juventus
    30.1 PPG | 3.2 BLK
    Kareem still has a terrifying ballot profile. The team result is the only thing keeping this from being a live No. 1 argument.
  3. 3Jack Sikma, Chelsea
    23.6 PPG | 9.1 RPG | 3.5 APG
    Chelsea are sitting on the promotion line, and that context lifts Sikma's already stable production.
  4. 4Steve Johnson, Sheffield United
    22.1 PPG | .685 FG | 23.7 PER
    If he had bigger name recognition, people would already be yelling this case.
  5. 5Larry Nance, Aston Villa
    20.4 PPG | 6.3 RPG | 1.7 BLK
    Production earns the spot. Villa's team context caps the climb.
Tier 3 Ballot
Conference League MVP Race
  1. 1Joe Barry Carroll, Paris Saint-Germain
    30.7 PPG | 8.1 RPG | 2.0 BLK | 27.4 PER
    Carroll has the strongest total star line in the tier and enough team support to keep the case serious.
  2. 2Kelly Tripucka, Barcelona
    31.3 PPG | strong shooting splits
    Tripucka's scoring is brutally good. The cleanest ding is availability relative to the rest of the field.
  3. 3World B. Free, Inter Milan
    28.1 PPG | 4.1 APG | hottest team month
    This is the momentum slot. If Inter finish on top, Free can absolutely win this race.
  4. 4David Thompson, FL Fart
    30.3 PPG | efficient scoring
    The raw scoring is MVP-grade for a team sitting one game off the promotion line.
  5. 5Calvin Natt, Manchester City
    29.2 PPG | 10.3 RPG | 3.2 APG
    Natt has the best if-team-context-were-kinder case in the entire package.

The deciding factor going into the next sim is not raw talent. It is whether these frontrunners can keep their team stories aligned with their stat lines. That is how good candidacies become winning ballots.
