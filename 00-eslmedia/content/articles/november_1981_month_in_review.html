Month In Review - November 1981

The regular season bites back

November gave the league its first hard regular-season truths: Sheffield surged, Aston Villa sank, Richmond led, and the promotion lines sharpened fast.

Graham TrentNovember 1981 monthly review

November was the month the league stopped sounding hypothetical. AFC Richmond built the strongest Tier 1 case. Sheffield United and Marseille turned Tier 2 into a promotion fight with real pace. Barcelona took the single Tier 3 promotion line. Aston Villa, meanwhile, made the bottom of the table impossible to ignore.

The Loudest Game
Sheffield United 116, Aston Villa 74
A 42-point margin was the month's biggest blowout and the cleanest single-game image of Tier 2's split personality. Sheffield looked like a promotion team. Villa looked like a club searching for the floor.
The Team Of The Month

Sheffield United gets the nod because 6-1 with a plus-120 differential is impossible to soften. The W5 streak matters, but the way those wins arrived matters more. Rickey Green's team did not just climb the table; it changed the temperature around every promotion conversation.

The Shock Result

Crystal Palace beating AFC Richmond was the kind of upset that keeps a poor month from becoming flat. Palace is still in relegation trouble at 2-6, but that win over the Tier 1 leader forces every opponent to treat the club as dangerous, even while the table treats it as vulnerable.

The Tightest Finish

Tottenham Hotspur's one-point win over Manchester City gave November its closest finish and gave Tier 3 another layer of irritation. Tottenham remains at the bottom of the board, but narrow wins matter when the tier has only one promotion spot and every team is trying to stay relevant long enough for a run.

The Coldest File

Aston Villa's 0-8 month is the hardest fact in the league. Monaco is nearby in trouble, Ajax is sliding, and Manchester United has its own Tier 1 mess, but no team owns a more direct problem than Villa: no wins, a minus-120 differential, and no soft explanation left.

The month ends with clean leaders and messy chases. Richmond, Sheffield, Marseille, and Barcelona have the lines everyone else wants. The next month is about whether those lines harden or finally start moving.
