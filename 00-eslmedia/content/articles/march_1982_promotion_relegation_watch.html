March Promotion and Relegation Watch: The Lines Got Messy

The format is simple: two down in Tier 1, two up and one down in Tier 2, and one up from Tier 3. The actual races are not simple at all.

Nina ValeMarch 1982 race watch

March did not settle the pressure lines. It made them more specific. Tier 1 survival is now a monthly-form argument because Crystal Palace, Brighton, Manchester United, and Atletico all gave the board reasons to worry. Tier 2 promotion is a four-team fight if you use performance rather than seed alone. Tier 3 is the cleanest: FL Fart own the line, but PSG and Manchester City are close enough to make every stumble matter.

Tier 1 Relegation
The line is not just the bottom two anymore
Two down | Palace 3-5, Brighton 3-5, United 3-4, Atletico 2-7 in March
Crystal Palace are the listed line team, but March makes this a four-club sweat. Atletico had the worst month at 2-7 and minus-111, Brighton lost three straight, and United remain buried overall despite a decent 3-4 sim. Palace helped themselves late with wins over Bayern and Brighton, but minus-53 for the month keeps them in the danger band.
Tier 2 Promotion
Benfica still control it, but Juventus and Chelsea made the line noisy
Two up | Benfica 5-3, Juventus 5-3, Chelsea 5-5, Marseille 4-3
Benfica remain the safest promotion case because the full-season record is 25-12. Juventus are the cleanest active chaser after a 5-3 month and a 111-76 win over Chelsea. Chelsea are on the line in monthly form because they played ten games and split them, but the 118-81 win over Benfica proves they can change the table in one night. Marseille are just behind, and a plus-34 month keeps them live rather than decorative.
Tier 2 Relegation
Ajax and Monaco are sweating, but Villa finally complicated the floor
One down | Ajax 3-5, Monaco 3-5, Villa 4-4 in March
Ajax sit on the monthly line and closed with three straight losses. Monaco have the same 3-5 record and the same minus-25 margin, so there is no comfort there. Aston Villa still own the worst season file, but March was not a dead team month: they beat Benfica, edged Sheffield, and hammered Ajax. That does not erase 9-28, but it changes the tone.
Tier 3 Promotion
FL Fart hold the one ticket, PSG and City are close enough to bother them
One up | FL Fart 6-1, PSG 6-2, Manchester City 6-3
FL Fart own the line because they had the best month and beat both Barcelona and Inter. PSG have the star push with Joe Barry Carroll and a 6-2 month. Manchester City are the awkward third party after going 6-3 and beating Barcelona by 25. Inter and Barcelona still have strong season cases, but March's race line belongs to the teams that actually surged.
What Changes Next

The next sim is about separation. Tier 1 needs one club to remove itself from the relegation group. Tier 2 needs Chelsea or Marseille to turn one huge result into a run. Tier 3 needs FL Fart to protect the line against a PSG team whose best player is now the tier's leading MVP argument.
