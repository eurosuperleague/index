Promotion and Relegation Watch: January Makes the Lines Easier to See

The rules are no longer theoretical. January did not lock any race, but it made the pressure lines easier to read.

Nina ValeJanuary 1982 pressure map

This is the point in the season where the rules stop sounding theoretical. Tier 1 sends two clubs down. Tier 2 sends two up and one down. Tier 3 sends one up. January did not lock any race, but it made the pressure lines easier to see.

Tier 1 Relegation: United Clear, Brighton Still Exposed
Two relegation spots - Manchester United 1-7 January, Brighton 3-4 January

Manchester United are still the clearest danger case after a 1-7 sim and a minus-98 point differential. Brighton are the other live worry because the Richmond demolition still hangs over the month. Crystal Palace used January to move away from the line, which makes the bottom feel less crowded and more direct.

Tier 2 Promotion: Chelsea Owns The Slot, Sheffield Owns The Form
Two promotion spots - Chelsea on the line, Sheffield United 6-4 January

Chelsea hold the second promotion place for now, but Sheffield United were the better January team and Benfica sit close enough to punish any wobble. Monaco's four-game winning streak gives the chase a dangerous extra voice.

Tier 2 Relegation: Villa Still Carries The Worst File
One relegation spot - Aston Villa 5-18 overall, Ajax directional arrow up

Aston Villa's upset of Marseille matters, but it does not erase the table. Ajax are alive after a three-game winning streak. Marseille's 3-4 month is the weird subplot because the tier leader suddenly has a current-form problem it should not be carrying.

Tier 3 Promotion: Inter Has The Door
One promotion spot - Inter Milan 6-2 January, FL Fart and Barcelona one game back

Inter Milan own the line because they did more than win. They dominated. FL Fart remain close because they keep banking solid weeks, and Barcelona remain dangerous because their scoring can still tilt a week. This is the cleanest race on the board: one club on the line, two close enough to punish any wobble.

The Read

The promotion fights are still editorial rather than mathematical. The relegation fights are less polite. United need proof of life, Brighton need consistency, Villa need more than one upset, and Inter need to prove January was control rather than noise.
