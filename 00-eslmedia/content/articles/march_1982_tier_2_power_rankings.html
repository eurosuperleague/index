March Tier 2 Power Rankings: Benfica Holds, The Pack Closes

The top team survived a weird month, but Juventus, Marseille, and Chelsea all gave the promotion race more teeth.

Nina ValeMarch 1982 power board

Tier 2 is tighter than the standings headline suggests. Benfica still own the best full-season profile, but March took away the feeling that they were above the room. Sheffield slipped, Juventus climbed, Marseille rebuilt some credibility, and Chelsea delivered the loudest single result of the month by beating Benfica by 37.

Europa League Board - March Form Check
  1. 1Benfica
    No change from No. 1 | 25-12 | 5-3 in March | Moses Malone
    Benfica stay first because 25-12 with Moses Malone still beats every rival's total case. The month was not clean: Aston Villa upset them and Chelsea blasted them 118-81. But Benfica answered with four straight wins and a 130-111 road win over Sheffield, which keeps the top line theirs.
  2. 2Juventus
    Up 1 from No. 3 | 21-15 | 5-3 in March | Kareem Abdul-Jabbar
    Juventus edge up because the form and the star case are both real. Kareem remains a top-two MVP argument in the tier, and the 111-76 win over Chelsea was a reminder that Juventus can turn a promotion rival into a bad night quickly.
  3. 3Sheffield United
    Down 1 from No. 2 | 23-13 | 3-4 in March | Steve Johnson
    Sheffield are still too good to slide further, but March was their first proper wobble. They lost by one at Aston Villa, lost by 19 at home to Benfica, and finished the sim under water by five points. The season differential is still excellent; the momentum is not.
  4. 4Marseille
    Up 1 from No. 5 | 21-14 | 4-3 in March | Robert Parish
    Marseille did enough to repair February's damage. The 117-99 win over Juventus was the best evidence, Robert Parish still anchors a serious interior profile, and the plus-34 month looked like a team remembering its early-season identity.
  5. 5Chelsea
    Down 1 from No. 4 | 19-18 | 5-5 in March | Jack Sikma
    Chelsea were both impressive and annoying, which is why they stay fifth. Beating Benfica by 37 is the biggest result in the tier. Losing badly to Juventus is the counterargument. A 5-5 month with a plus-3 margin says line-team, not leap-team.
  6. 6Monaco
    Up 1 from No. 7 | 14-21 | 3-5 in March | Purvis Short
    Monaco rise only because the bottom is crowded. They beat Aston Villa, split enough of the messy games to stay alive, and finished with the same March differential as Ajax. The problem is that minus-246 on the season still screams fragile.
  7. 7Ajax
    Down 1 from No. 6 | 13-24 | 3-5 in March | Cliff Robinson
    Ajax beat Monaco by 17, then still ended the month on a three-game losing streak. That is the whole frustration. There is enough talent to create a useful night, but the sustained profile remains too loose for comfort.
  8. 8Aston Villa
    No change from No. 8 | 9-28 | 4-4 in March | Bill Laimbeer
    Villa stay last because the season hole is enormous, not because March was empty. They beat Benfica, edged Sheffield by one, and hammered Ajax 122-93. That is genuine improvement. It is also still attached to a 9-28 record and a minus-268 season differential.
The Read

Benfica are still first, but March made the race feel less obedient. Juventus look like the cleanest climber, Marseille are trustworthy again, and Chelsea remain the team that can scare anyone without yet convincing the board for a full month.
