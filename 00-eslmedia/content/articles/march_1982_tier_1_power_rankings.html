March Tier 1 Power Rankings: Milan Made The Jump

Richmond and Bayern are still almost even, but Milan had the month that changed the board and Atletico had the month that broke trust.

Nina ValeMarch 1982 power board

Tier 1 finally has movement that is not cosmetic. AFC Richmond take the top line because Magic Johnson's team matched Bayern at 23-13 and had the cleaner March. AC Milan made the biggest meaningful rise by going 6-2, beating Bayern, and closing on four straight. Atletico Madrid are the opposite story: a 2-7 month and seven-game skid turned a comfortable February ranking into a warning label.

Champions League Board - March Form Check
  1. 1AFC Richmond
    Up 1 from No. 2 | 23-13 | 5-2 in March | Magic Johnson
    Richmond get the edge because the month was stronger than Bayern's and the season file is now level on record. The 119-98 win at Atletico showed the ceiling, and Magic's 24.0 points, 7.4 assists, and 30.1 PER still give this team the best single organizing force in the tier. The only caution is the 124-105 loss to United at the end.
  2. 2Bayern Munich
    Down 1 from No. 1 | 23-13 | 4-5 in March | Bernard King, Kevin McHale
    Bayern do not fall far because the overall case remains elite, but a losing month matters. They still crushed Manchester United by 34, yet losses to AC Milan and Crystal Palace left the board with enough doubt to move them off the top line. This is a drop, not a panic.
  3. 3AC Milan
    Up 2 from No. 5 | 22-14 | 6-2 in March | Larry Bird, George Gervin
    This is the rise that actually changed Tier 1. Milan posted the best March in the tier, finished plus-72, beat Richmond and Bayern, and closed by hammering Atletico 119-97. Larry Bird's scoring and playmaking make the surge feel more sustainable than a random hot week.
  4. 4Real Madrid
    Up 2 from No. 6 | 19-16 | 5-1 in March | Sidney Moncrief
    Real finally turned their underlying profile into wins. A 5-1 month and five straight victories pull them out of the danger haze, while Sidney Moncrief keeps giving the board a two-way reason to believe. The 115-99 win over Brighton was the practical result; the bigger story is that Real stopped leaking points in the table.
  5. 5Crystal Palace
    Down 1 from No. 4 | 17-19 | 3-5 in March | Artis Gilmore
    Palace beat Bayern 120-104 and closed with two wins, so this is not a full reversal. It is still a drop because the month was minus-53 and included too many nights where the defense vanished. Palace remain dangerous, but February's clean rise got messier.
  6. 6Atletico Madrid
    Down 3 from No. 3 | 17-19 | 2-7 in March | James Donaldson
    This is the faller. Atletico lost seven straight, finished March at minus-111, and were beaten by 33 at Bayern before Milan ended the month with another 22-point loss. The Real Madrid win was useful, but one good result cannot cover a month this heavy.
  7. 7Brighton
    No change from No. 7 | 15-21 | 3-5 in March | Kiki Vandeweghe
    Brighton stay seventh because the lower tier of the board did not offer a cleaner answer. The 125-115 win at Milan was genuinely strong, and the monthly differential was not disastrous, but the three-game losing streak keeps them in survival mode.
  8. 8Manchester United
    No change from No. 8 | 7-28 | 3-4 in March | Walter Davis
    United had a better sim than their season deserves, including a 124-105 win over Richmond and a 117-92 win over Palace. That matters. The problem is that 7-28 is still too deep a hole, and the 34-point loss to Bayern reminds everyone why this spot remains theirs.
The Read

Milan are the team whose March changed belief. Real Madrid earned relief. Atletico and Bayern are the teams whose next sim will decide whether this was just turbulence or the start of a bigger slide.
