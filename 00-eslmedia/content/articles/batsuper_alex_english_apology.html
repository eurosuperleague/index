Crystal Palace - Fan Voice - 1981 Regular Season

I Would Like To Apologize To Alex English. Unfortunately I Still Have Eyes

batsuper would like the record to show that Crystal Palace kept winning, Alex English kept cooking, and the previous round of yelling now requires an embarrassing but genuine correction.

batsuper Apology Column · Crystal Palace Fan Voice

I would like to begin with a sincere apology to Alex English because, regrettably, the man kept going out there and making me look ridiculous. Not a fake apology. Not one of those apology notes where a man says "if anyone was offended" and then immediately starts clearing his throat. A real apology. I got loud, I got theatrical, and I got a little too comfortable talking like every tough jumper was proof of moral collapse. Meanwhile Alex is sitting there averaging 20.6 points a night on 48.6 percent shooting and continuing to behave like one of the best scorers in the file.

And now Palace has gone and made the whole thing worse for me by winning. Not once. Repeatedly. This club just went 5-2 in January, ripped off four straight, beat Atletico Madrid twice, beat Bayern Munich, beat Manchester United, and then closed the month by blasting AC Milan 129-111 like they were settling a personal score. At some point, even a committed overreactor has to sit down, drink some water, and admit the team is actually playing good basketball.

"Palace kept stacking wins, Alex kept stacking buckets, and suddenly I was the only man left on earth still arguing with the box score."

Alex English especially has spent the past two weeks dragging me by the collar through the correction process. He put 29 on Bayern on January 15. He came back with 30 and seven assists against Manchester United on January 21. Then he hung 24 on AC Milan in only 26 minutes in that 129-111 demolition on January 30. That is not me getting tricked by a hot quarter. That is a man putting together a run and forcing an apology out of a stubborn person.

So yes, Alex, I am sorry. You did not personally force me to witness every leaning 19-footer with 17 seconds still on the clock. And, much more annoyingly for me, you responded to my public irritation by helping drag Palace out of the danger fog and into a stretch that looks real. A grown man cannot see four straight wins, a 7-3 last ten, and this kind of scoring run, then continue pretending he is the only adult in the room. Or rather he can, but then he becomes embarrassing.

Official Apology Structure
The Three Terms Of Apology
01
I apologize for the volume, the dramatics, and the way I talked like I had discovered fire by noticing a contested jumper.
02
I acknowledge that Alex English has been lighting good teams up lately and making my previous tone look hasty at best and hysterical at worst.
03
I reserve the right to grab the armrest again if the next possession ends in a double-covered fadeaway with 15 on the clock.
Where We Stand

The thing I missed while performing a one-man dramatic reading of my own frustration is that Palace's offense recently has looked less like chaos and more like pressure. The January file says 757 points in seven games, a plus-44 margin, and a 7-3 mark in the last ten overall. That is not the profile of a team spiraling into self-indulgent nonsense. That is the profile of a team landing punches.

And Alex has been part of those answers. Not the whole answer, because Artis Gilmore has been huge and the team has defended better too. But Alex has absolutely been one of the reasons this stretch feels different. If I made it sound like he was just a roaming hazard in sneakers, that was unfair to both the player and the very obvious evidence piling up right in front of me.

Now, because I believe in honesty between friends, I will also say this: the occasional ambitious elbow jumper still hits my nervous system like an unexplained bank charge. I am working on myself. Growth is messy. Sincerity and leftover jump-shot trauma can coexist.

Closing Statement

Alex, if you are reading this, I respect your game, I respect the month you just had, and I respect the fact that Crystal Palace right now looks a lot more dangerous than doomed. That deserved to be said loudly, and preferably before you kept turning good opponents into evidence against me.

I hope this apology is received in the spirit it is offered: sweaty, overdue, and real. Palace is playing well. Alex English is playing well. I should have said that with my whole chest instead of acting like every difficult make was a sign of civil decline.

And if the next Palace game contains one completely unnecessary double-covered pull-up from 18 feet, I promise to process it with far more maturity than before. Maybe not immediately. But eventually. Give me a quarter.
