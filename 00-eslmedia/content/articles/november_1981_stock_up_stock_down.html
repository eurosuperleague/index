Stock Up / Stock Down - November 1981

Sheffield and Villa define the stock report

The month produced three clean risers, three obvious fallers, and a reminder that point differential can make a record feel louder or uglier.

Damon CrossNovember 1981 stock report

Some stock reports require nuance. This one starts with a hammer. Sheffield United is 6-1 with a plus-120 differential. Aston Villa is 0-8 with a minus-120 differential. That is not a column; that is a warning label taped to both ends of the league.

Stock Up
Sheffield United
6-1 | +120 | W5
This is the cleanest rise in the league. Sheffield is not merely winning; it is winning by margins that change the way the promotion race gets discussed.
Marseille
6-1 | +87 | W4
Marseille would be the headline in most months. Robert Parish gives the roster a serious anchor, and the W4 streak keeps the club level with Sheffield on record.
AFC Richmond
6-2 | +79 | Tier 1 leader
Richmond's stock is still up even after the Bayern loss. A top-tier team can take a hit and remain the standard when the full body of work is this strong.
Stock Down
Aston Villa
0-8 | -120 | L8
There is no clever way around this. Villa has not won, has been outscored heavily, and already sits alone in the Tier 2 relegation chair.
Monaco
1-5 | -124 | danger nearby
Monaco has one more win than Villa and somehow an even worse differential. That is how a team becomes a quiet crisis.
Manchester United
2-5 | -100 | L3
United's slide is not just about losses. It is about the shape of them. Minus-100 through seven games makes the trapdoor feel less like a threat and more like a location.

The wild card is Chelsea, which did not make either list because the record and numbers are arguing with each other. Four wins and three losses says ordinary. Plus-63 says danger. That contradiction is worth saving for the next board.
