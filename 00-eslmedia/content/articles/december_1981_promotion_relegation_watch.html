Promotion and Relegation Watch: December Tightens the Trapdoors

The cleanest race is in Marseille; the ugliest one is under Manchester United; the strangest one is Sporting CP holding a promotion line with a losing overall record.

Graham TrentDecember 1981 pressure map

By December, the table stops being decorative. It begins to make demands. Manchester United has lost seven straight and now lives in the Tier 1 relegation conversation not as a surprise guest, but as a tenant. Real Madrid has enough pedigree to make the line feel temporary, yet the current form says the danger is not theoretical.

Tier 1 Relegation: United Stuck, Madrid Exposed
Two relegation spots - Manchester United 0-7 December, Real Madrid 3-6 December

United's minus-86 month is the number that changes the tone. Walter Davis can still give the club professional scoring, but nothing about the current file suggests safety. Madrid is more complicated: Sidney Moncrief keeps the roster credible, but a minus-68 December has dragged them onto the line. Crystal Palace and Brighton are close enough that one week can rearrange the entire bottom half.

Tier 2 Promotion: Marseille Is Clear, The Second Seat Is Not
Two promotion spots - Marseille 7-0 December, Juventus on the live line

Marseille has stopped asking for promotion consideration and started behaving like it is owed. Robert Parish is the stable center of a 13-1 overall case. The second slot is messier. Juventus has the current line after a 5-3 month, Benfica is one result back, and Ajax has just enough momentum to be annoying from below.

Tier 2 Relegation: Villa Escapes Zero, Not Trouble
One relegation spot - Aston Villa and Chelsea both below comfort

Aston Villa finally has wins, which matters, but Bill Laimbeer's side still carries the worst overall profile in the tier. Chelsea is the more interesting problem. Jack Sikma's team has a positive season margin and a losing record, the sort of contradiction that keeps coaches employed and fans irritated. Monaco remains close enough to be pulled back in.

Tier 3 Promotion: Sporting Has The Line, Inter Has The Argument
One promotion spot - Sporting CP 5-3 December, Inter Milan plus-49 December

Sporting CP owns the current line because December rewarded momentum. Inter Milan may own the stronger case if one cares about point differential. Valencia is only a step behind, and Barcelona's December wobble turned what looked like control into a queue. This is the most volatile race because there is only one door.

The Read

The promotion fights are still close enough to be editorial rather than mathematical. The relegation fights are less polite. United has to change the conversation immediately, Madrid has to prove the slump is not identity, and Chelsea has to turn good margins into actual standings relief.
