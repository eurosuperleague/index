Europa League Power Rankings: Chelsea earns the first top line

The Europa board is less top-heavy than the Champions League table, but the opening six-game sample already gives Chelsea separation, Benfica a real chase case, and Aston Villa a hole to climb out of.

If the Champions League feels built on brand gravity, the Europa League board feels built on structure. There is less superstar overload here, but more teams with a believable route to solid basketball. The standings file gives one obvious opening truth: Chelsea owns the hottest line at 5-1. The roster data fills in the rest of the picture. Benfica has the tier's biggest singular hammer in Moses Malone, Marseille has enough veteran ballast to stay high, and the bottom of the board is already punishing any team that has not found a shape in the opening sample.

The Board
Europa League Opening six-game sample
  1. 1Chelsea
    Last six: 5-1 | Core: Jack Sikma, Jim Paxson, Robert Reid
    The simplest call on any of the three boards. Chelsea has the best record in the section and a top trio strong enough to support it. Sikma gives them a center line with real authority, and the perimeter scoring balance keeps them from leaning on one answer too heavily.
  2. 2Benfica
    Last six: 4-2 | Core: Moses Malone, Mychal Thompson, Ray Williams
    Malone is the biggest single talent swing in the tier, and that matters in an early ranking. Benfica may not yet own the cleanest record line, but any team with the best player on the floor that often is going to live near the top until proven otherwise.
  3. 3Marseille
    Last six: 4-2 | Core: Robert Parish, Gus Williams, Jamaal Wilkes
    Marseille feels older and steadier than flashy. Parish gives them an interior baseline, Gus Williams gives them pace, and the 4-2 opening keeps them in the top line conversation without much argument.
  4. 4Juventus
    Last six: 3-3 | Core: Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Rolando Blackman, John Lucas
    A .500 start is not enough to break into the top three, but Kareem still changes the tone of any matchup. Juventus is the kind of team that can sit fourth on a ranking and still scare the clubs above them if the rhythm sharpens.
  5. 5Sheffield United
    Last six: 3-3 | Core: Rickey Green, Bill Cartwright, Billy Knight
    This is the league's most conventional middle. Sheffield looks balanced, competent, and still a little ordinary. That is not a criticism so much as a reminder that this group still needs a signature trait before it can climb.
  6. 6Monaco
    Last six: 3-3 | Core: Purvis Short, Danny Ainge, Wayne Cooper
    Monaco is harder to slot than Sheffield because the talent base is thinner, but Purvis Short and Danny Ainge give them enough offensive credibility to stay out of the bottom two. They are competitive without yet feeling threatening.
  7. 7Ajax
    Last six: 2-4 | Core: Cliff Robinson, Maurice Cheeks, James Edwards
    The top of Ajax's roster is respectable, especially with Cheeks organizing the floor, but the opening results are too soft to ignore. There is room for a rebound here, just not enough evidence yet to rank them above the neutral clubs.
  8. 8Aston Villa
    Last six: 0-6 | Core: Bill Laimbeer, Larry Nance, Dudley Bradley
    This one is non-negotiable. Villa is the only team in the entire three-tier snapshot that opens 0-6, and even a front line with Laimbeer and Nance cannot soften that. They do not just need a win; they need a reset.
The Shape Of The Tier

The first thing that jumps off the Europa board is how little room there is between second and sixth. Benfica, Marseille, Juventus, Sheffield United, and Monaco all have some version of a workable identity. The gap is not really about whether they belong in the race. It is about whether they can turn that identity into week-to-week pressure. Chelsea has done that earliest, which is why the top spot is theirs without much drama.

The team with the easiest path upward is Juventus. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar is still the kind of centerpiece who can bend a ranking in a hurry, and a 3-3 opening is not a meaningful hole. The team under the heaviest pressure is Aston Villa, because 0-6 instantly changes the tone of every conversation around a roster. Larry Nance and Bill Laimbeer are too recognizable to stay buried forever, but the board cannot hand out sympathy points just because the names look familiar.

That is what makes this tier compelling. It does not have one royal club choking the oxygen out of the room. It has a live race, a real bottom, and several teams one decent week away from changing the conversation. For a first power-ranking edition, that is exactly what you want.
