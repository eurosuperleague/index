Europa League Power Rankings: Chelsea leads the opening board

Tier 2 has the cleanest top case on the site. Chelsea owns the strongest preseason file, Benfica is the closest threat, and the rest of the ladder quickly turns into a fight between stable middles and teams already dragging too much damage.

Europa was the easiest tier to rank because Chelsea had the simplest evidence pile in the entire site. A 5-1 record, a plus-71 differential, and a five-game win streak should make the opening No. 1 call feel almost boring. Behind Chelsea, though, the board becomes more interesting: Benfica has the strongest chase case, Marseille looks dependable, and the middle is already splitting into clubs you trust and clubs you merely tolerate.

The Board

What Actually Changed

The top three are straightforward because they do not need narrative gymnastics. Chelsea still has the strongest complete body of work. Benfica is the team with the strongest upward pressure. Marseille remains the most believable third-place club on the site because the team reads coherent whether or not the newest result file gave it a headline.

The live argument is Sheffield versus Juventus. Sheffield's record and differential are more stable than people want to admit, while Juventus is still living partly off theoretical ceiling. That does not mean Sheffield has the higher postseason ceiling. It means the opening board should rank the team that has done less to sabotage its own case.

The team I remain unsure about is Monaco. A neutral record with a brutal point differential usually tells you something ugly is hiding under the tablecloth. If Monaco steadies next month, the sixth slot will look conservative. If not, this may end up reading generous.
