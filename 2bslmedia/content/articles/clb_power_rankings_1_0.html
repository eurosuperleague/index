Champions League Power Rankings: Palace opens on top

This first preseason board leans on total form, point differential, and the strength of each team's best wins. Crystal Palace gets the opening No. 1, Bayern and Richmond are right behind, and AC Milan already has more questions than a star roster should invite.

Preseason rankings are awkward by design. The sample is still small enough to tempt overreaction, but the shape of the tier is already visible if you trust the full file more than the loudest single night. Crystal Palace gets the first Champions League top line because the club paired a 4-2 record with the best point differential in the section, while Bayern Munich and AFC Richmond gave themselves the strongest chase cases behind them.

  8. 8Manchester United

    United is the easiest bottom call in the section. The roster has enough quality to recover later, but the preseason file gives no honest reason to place them anywhere else today.
Three Real Takeaways

Palace is the opening No. 1 because this board should reward the best total case, not the most charismatic one. A 4-2 mark with a plus-45 differential is the cleanest line in the tier, and there is enough two-way balance in the roster to make that profile feel sustainable rather than decorative.

The team everyone will argue about is Brighton. That is fair. A single 22-point win should not erase the earlier uneven work, but it should matter when the team you just hit is AC Milan and the clubs in front of you are all carrying some kind of unresolved doubt. Brighton is the team I am still least certain about on the top half of this board, which is exactly why the placement is interesting.

The toughest call is Milan, and the doubt case is still Madrid. Milan's stars make the club too dangerous to dismiss, but the sheet is grim enough now that every "wait until they settle" argument has to carry less weight. Madrid is less dramatic and maybe more frustrating: the roster keeps suggesting fourth, the evidence keeps suggesting sixth. That tension is going to define the next edition.
