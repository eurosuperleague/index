Europa League Awards Predictions: Big Men, Big Stakes, No Apologies

This tier is drowning in frontcourt authority, so the Europa awards board starts with brute force in the MVP race, brute force in the DPOY race, and a first team loaded with grown-man basketball.

Damon Cross Europa League awards preview

If you want finesse, go somewhere else. The Europa League is a heavyweight division, and the first name I am writing down for MVP is Moses Malone. Not because it sounds good. Because it is true. Benfica has him at 148 overall, one of the cleanest superstar numbers outside the top tier, and unlike some bloated award debates people create for television, this one does not require acrobatics. He is the best centerpiece in the tier, he is attached to a team already living near the top line, and he is exactly the kind of player voters talk themselves into by January because the production feels unavoidable.

MVP Prediction
Moses Malone, Benfica
Elite top-end talent, elite rebounding profile, and a club already positioned to be taken seriously. That is how MVP campaigns get oxygen fast.
Defensive Player Of The Year
Robert Parish, Marseille
Jack Sikma has a case. Kareem has a case. Maurice Cheeks will have the guards screaming. I am still taking Parish because size, rim protection, and contender credibility all meet in one place.

Where The Drama Lives

The toughest thing about this board is not finding stars. It is surviving the center logjam with your dignity intact. Jack Sikma is sitting there on a Chelsea club that already looks coherent and dangerous. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar is still Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, which means leaving him off any top line feels vaguely disrespectful even when the logic is sound. Cliff Robinson and Maurice Cheeks give Ajax two of the sneakiest high-end cases in the tier. This is not a weak league. This is a league where grown men are going to miss first team because another grown man is standing in their way with a cleaner roster argument.

And yes, I know some of you are going to push back on Rickey Green making my first team over bigger brand names. Go look at the profile. He has the ball pressure, the guard control, and the overall two-way punch to deserve this conversation. Europa is not only about celebrity. It is about who actually bends games in this tier.

But the fundamental truth is simple: if Benfica remains strong, Moses is going to dominate this award cycle. If Marseille hardens into a contender, Parish is going to be impossible to ignore on the defensive side. And if Chelsea really is the steadiest team in the division, Paxson and Reid are going to live on every serious ballot. The Europa League is a fight, but the outlines are already there for anyone willing to stop whispering and start watching.
