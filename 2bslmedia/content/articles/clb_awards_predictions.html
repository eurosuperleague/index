Champions League Awards Predictions: Start With Magic, Then Start Arguing

The biggest tier deserves the biggest declarations, and the Champions League already has a clear MVP favorite, a bruising DPOY race, and a loaded first-team ballot.

Damon Cross Champions League awards preview

Let me be crystal clear: if you are doing preseason Champions League awards and you are not starting with Magic Johnson, you are overthinking the room. AFC Richmond already looks like one of the safest elite bets in the tier, and unlike some of these glamorous names living off reputation, his team actually fits around him. That matters. MVP is not a museum exhibit. It is about the player most likely to bend the entire division around his existence, and right now that is Magic.

MVP Prediction
Magic Johnson, AFC Richmond
Best player in the tier, cleanest team ecosystem near the top of the board, and the broadest all-around impact. That is the formula.
Defensive Player Of The Year
Artis Gilmore, Crystal Palace
I respect Michael Cooper. I respect Julius Erving. But if we are talking about a defender who can erase mistakes and still keep a contender upright, Gilmore has the strongest lane.

The Real Fight

Now here is where this tier gets delicious. Crystal Palace is going to have something to say about both major individual awards, because Gilmore gives them the cleanest interior force and Darrell Griffith's 135 overall is not decorative. AC Milan, meanwhile, has enough star power to bully the entire first-team conversation. Larry Bird and Julius Erving on one preseason ballot is not extravagance. It is honesty. If that team ever aligns its record with its talent, this column is going to look conservative.

The hardest cut for me was leaving Michael Cooper off the top defensive line in favor of Gilmore for the actual trophy. Cooper is absolutely an all-defense caliber name, and Richmond's winning environment is going to make his case louder every week. But DPOY is not always about the most versatile guy. Sometimes it is about the man who makes the paint feel closed for business. That is Gilmore's argument, and it is a powerful one.

And let me say this before somebody starts clutching pearls over Real Madrid or Bayern Munich: this is a prediction, not a lifetime achievement banquet. Sidney Moncrief, Bernard King, and Dan Roundfield all have real cases to crack the first team if their clubs rise. But on day one, this league still belongs to Magic's gravity, Palace's muscle, and Milan's absurd star wattage. That is the board, and if you do not like it, wonderful. That means the season is already doing its job.
