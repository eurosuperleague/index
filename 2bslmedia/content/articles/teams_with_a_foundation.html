Roster Building - Long View - Season 13

Which Teams Actually Have a Foundation?

The early standings tell you who has started well, but the more useful question is which clubs have built something that should still make sense after the first hot streak cools off.

Nina Vale Scouting File · Season 13
4-2Richmond Start
5-1Chelsea Start
5-1Man City Start

A foundation is not just a record. It is not even just talent. It is a record supported by age, role clarity, and enough financial or structural flexibility that the club can absorb the next problem without becoming a different team entirely. That is the standard, and it narrows the field quickly.

AFC Richmond
Why the base is real
4-2 start · young star core · huge market
Richmond does not just have Magic Johnson at age 20. It also has Tom Chambers at 20, Kurt Rambis at 21, and enough surrounding depth that development does not have to happen in chaos.

The best indicator here is not merely that Richmond has started 4-2. It is that the club’s timeline is layered. Magic Johnson already looks like a player who changes a franchise’s ceiling, but the roster beneath him is not barren. Chambers, Rambis, Michael Cooper, Brad Davis and Vinnie Johnson all sit in the age range where improvement is not theoretical.

That matters because the club can win now without emptying the future to do it. Richmond has a huge market, a win-now owner profile, and multiple rotation players young enough to appreciate in value. That is what a stable contender looks like before the league has fully admitted it.

Chelsea
Why the record is supported
5-1 start · veteran spine · youth runway
Jack Sikma, Jim Paxson, and David Greenwood give Chelsea present-day credibility. Valerio Sagredo, Gene Banks, and Jim Smith make the long-term math more forgiving.

Chelsea’s case is different from Richmond’s. It is less about one obvious superstar axis and more about balance between present and future. A 5-1 opening line can be noisy, but the roster does not read like a temporary trick. Sikma is only 22, Greenwood is 22, Paxson is 22, and Sagredo is already eighteen and relevant on a winning team.

What Chelsea has done well is avoid a dead middle. The club is not old enough to be decaying, and it is not so young that every important possession becomes an experiment. That is a better foundation than many prettier rosters claim to have.

Manchester City
Why the tier should take notice
5-1 start · deep guard line · low-cost youth
Jeff Lamp, Elston Turner, Carl Nicks, Allen Leavell, and Calvin Natt give City the kind of age curve that can turn a quick start into a longer stay.

Conference League teams rarely get the benefit of the doubt, but City has earned some. The roster has younger perimeter talent than most clubs in its section, and the contract table still leaves room for movement instead of locking the team into expensive decline. That is a meaningful distinction in a volatile tier.

Not every club with a good start has a foundation. Some simply have six useful nights. Richmond, Chelsea, and Manchester City all show stronger evidence than that. They have age on their side, structure underneath the headlines, and more than one path forward if the season gets complicated. That is usually enough to separate the real builds from the temporary ones.
