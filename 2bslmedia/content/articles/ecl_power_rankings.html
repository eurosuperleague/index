Conference League Power Rankings: Manchester City takes the first board

The Conference League section is lighter on household stars than the other two tiers, which makes the opening form lines matter even more. That is why Manchester City gets the first No. 1.

This board is the cleanest test of whether you trust results or names. The Conference League tier does not have a Richmond-level juggernaut or a Benfica-level singular star, so the six-game sample in standings.json carries more weight than usual. Manchester City gets rewarded for going 5-1. Paris Saint-Germain and FL Fart both have 4-2 openings that keep them near the top. And then the rest of the table starts sorting itself by which teams look coherent, which teams feel unfinished, and which ones already seem behind the pace.

The Board
Conference League Opening six-game sample
  1. 1Manchester City
    Last six: 5-1 | Core: Allen Leavell, Calvin Natt, Billy Ray Bates
    City does not own the biggest individual headliner in the section, but that is exactly the point of the ranking. They have the best form line, and in a tier where no one has separated on talent alone, that matters most.
  2. 2Paris Saint-Germain
    Last six: 4-2 | Core: Joe Barry Carroll, Otis Birdsong, Larry Smith
    PSG is not flashy, but the construction makes sense. Carroll gives them a middle, Birdsong gives them scoring competence, and the 4-2 start is strong enough to keep them within immediate striking distance of City.
  3. 3FL Fart
    Last six: 4-2 | Core: Alton Lister, Larry Drew, David Thompson
    The name is still doing a lot of damage to the seriousness of the conversation, but the record is not a joke. Lister and Drew are giving the club enough structure to earn a top-three slot on merit.
  4. 4Barcelona
    Last six: 3-3 | Core: Kelly Tripucka, Caldwell Jones, Darnell Valentine
    Barcelona sits first in the broad middle because Tripucka is the strongest offensive anchor of the .500 teams. There is still a lot of work to do, but the path to relevance is easier to see here than in the clubs below them.
  5. 5Tottenham Hotspur
    Last six: 3-3 | Core: John Long, Darryl Dawkins, Johnny Davis
    Spurs look solid without looking urgent. Dawkins gives them interior personality, but the roster as a whole still reads like a team that needs to prove it can dictate games instead of merely survive them.
  6. 6Valencia
    Last six: 2-4 | Core: Isiah Thomas, Kelvin Ransey, Mike Newlin
    Valencia probably has the most interesting single guard in the whole section. Isiah Thomas is enough reason to believe in a climb, but a 2-4 start is a real drag and the support around him still looks thin.
  7. 7Sporting CP
    Last six: 2-4 | Core: Buck Williams, Brian Taylor, Mike O'Koren
    Sporting gets the edge over Inter only because Buck Williams raises the floor. The opening form is still rough, and unless the perimeter play sharpens quickly this can become a long stretch of low-leverage basketball.
  8. 8Inter Milan
    Last six: 1-5 | Core: World B. Free, Clemon Johnson, Don Collins
    Inter owns the section's worst combination of form and stability. World B. Free can rescue individual nights, but a 1-5 line and a flat supporting cast make them the easiest bottom call on the board.
Why This Tier Feels Volatile

The Conference League ranking is probably the one most likely to change in a week. City deserves the top slot right now, but the case is built on outcomes more than fear factor. That means one cold stretch opens the door immediately. PSG and FL Fart both have enough early momentum to jump them, and even clubs sitting 3-3 or 2-4 are not buried because the gap in top-end talent is not overwhelming.

The most fascinating roster in the lower half belongs to Valencia. Isiah Thomas is the kind of lead guard who can force a correction by himself, and if this were a player-draft ranking instead of a team one, Valencia would look much higher. But power rankings are about the whole picture, and the whole picture still says 2-4 with too much of the burden resting on one elite creator.

That makes this section the most fun to revisit. In the Champions League, we are weighing giants. In the Europa League, we are tracking structure. Here, we are tracking volatility. The first board belongs to Manchester City because they are the club actually converting uncertainty into wins. The next one could look very different.
